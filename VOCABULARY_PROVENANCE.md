# Vocabulary Provenance & Legal Documentation

**Last Updated:** 2026-04-14  
**Project:** memory-palace-learner  
**Purpose:** This document establishes the lawful origin of all vocabulary data used in this application and provides a defensible chain of custody in the event of any intellectual property dispute.

---

## Executive Summary

All vocabulary content in this application is derived from **one or more of the following safe sources**:

1. **Factual linguistic frequency data** (not substantially copyrightable as creative expression)
2. **Wikidata Lexemes** (CC0 — public domain, no attribution required, commercial use permitted)
3. **Original algorithmic generation** (phonetic renderer, room-mapping scripts)
4. **Original local LLM generation** (contextual examples, conjugations, grammar notes — no third-party text copied)

**No AQA, Edexcel, GCSE, or other publisher vocabulary lists have been copied verbatim.**

---

## 1. Original Seed Vocabulary (955 words)

**File:** `src/data/italian-generated.ts` (imported from `camera-cafe-learner/data/italian-dictionary.json`)

**Origin:**
- The upstream `camera-cafe-learner` project sourced its base list from the **Crepaldi-2015 Italian Frequency Dataset**, a corpus-derived frequency list published in the linguistic literature (NoRaRe database).
- Corpus frequency lists represent **compilations of raw linguistic facts** (word forms and their observed frequencies in published texts). In most jurisdictions, purely factual compilations receive **thin or no copyright protection** because they lack the requisite originality/creative selection.
- The Crepaldi list was used as a **seed** to identify common Italian lemmas, not as a creative work to be reproduced.

**Transformation applied by this project:**
- Filtered to 955 high-frequency words
- Removed multi-word conversational phrases
- Replaced original machine pronunciations with a **custom pseudo-phonetic renderer** (see §3)
- Added emoji annotations from Unicode / open-source emoji datasets
- Re-mapped words into 18 thematic rooms using **original keyword-matching scripts** (see §4)

---

## 2. Wikidata Expansion (CC0 — Public Domain)

**License:** [CC0 1.0 Universal](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/)  
**Source:** [Wikidata SPARQL endpoint](https://query.wikidata.org/) — Italian lexemes (`dct:language wd:Q652`)

**What we use:**
- Italian lemmas (word forms)
- English glosses (where available on entity pages)
- Grammatical gender (masculine / feminine / none)
- Lexical category (noun, verb, etc.)

**Why this is safe:**
- Wikidata content is explicitly released into the public domain.
- CC0 waives all copyright and related rights.
- No attribution is legally required; commercial use is unrestricted.
- We access it via official SPARQL API with a descriptive User-Agent string.

**Fetch script:** `scripts/fetch-wikidata-vocab.cjs`

## 3. Algorithmic Pronunciations (Original Work)

**File:** `scripts/convert-vocab.cjs`

**Method:**
- Custom syllable splitter based on Italian vowel patterns (`a`, `e`, `i`, `o`, `u`, `à`, `è`, `é`, `ì`, `ò`, `ó`, `ù`)
- Stress heuristics (penultimate syllable default; antepenultimate for certain endings)
- Output format: hyphenated pseudo-phonetic strings (e.g. `ab-bo-na-MEN-to`, `a-KWA`)

**Status:** This is an **original software algorithm** written for this project. No pronunciation data was copied from dictionaries, textbooks, or other copyrighted sources.

---

## 4. Room Mapping (Original Work)

**Files:**
- `scripts/remap-vocab.cjs`
- `scripts/fix-entrance-hall.cjs`
- `src/data/rooms.ts`

**Method:**
- We created **original keyword taxonomies** (e.g. `fridge: ['latte','uova','burro','formaggio']`) based on common sense and everyday Italian life.
- Words were assigned to 18 rooms and their subrooms (zones) via automated string matching against English translations and Italian lemmas.
- `interiorVocab` positions are generated by a simple grid-layout algorithm.

**Status:** The thematic grouping and spatial layout are **original creative choices** made by the project authors and local LLMs consulted for suggestions. No room-to-word mapping was copied from any existing product.

---

## 5. AI-Generated Contextual Content (Original Work)

**System:** Local Ollama instance (`llama3.2:latest`, `qwen2.5:14b`, `qwen3:8b`)  
**Files:** `src/hooks/useOllama.ts`, `src/pages/RoomPage.tsx`

**Generated on demand:**
- Contextual example sentences (e.g. "The spoon is in the drawer")
- Verb conjugation tables (present, past, future)
- Grammar notes and exceptions

**Status:**
- All LLM output is **synthesized in real time** on the user's local machine.
- It is not a copy of any textbook, publisher list, or third-party dataset.
- Because it is generated locally and not downloaded from a cloud service, there are no Terms-of-Service restrictions on commercial use.

## 6. Explicitly Rejected / Avoided Sources

To maintain a clean IP position, the following sources were **consulted for reference only** and **never copied verbatim** into the application:

| Source | Reason for exclusion |
|--------|---------------------|
| AQA GCSE Italian Vocabulary List | © AQA — copyrighted exam material |
| Edexcel GCSE Italian Vocabulary Book | © Pearson — copyrighted exam material |
| Quizlet / Cramit flashcard decks | User-generated / often derived from copyrighted lists |
| Clozemaster word lists | Proprietary content |
| Printed dictionaries (Zanichelli, Garzanti, etc.) | Copyrighted definitions and examples |

When we needed additional words, we used **Wikidata (CC0)** and **local LLM generation** instead.

## 9. Contact / Maintenance

If any party believes a specific word, mapping, or example infringes their rights, please contact the project maintainers with:
1. The specific word or phrase in question
2. The allegedly infringed work
3. Evidence of substantial similarity beyond common dictionary facts

We will promptly investigate and, if warranted, replace the item with a Wikidata-sourced or LLM-generated alternative.
